Why we exist

Createwith began as a maker's workshop, a place with CNC machines, a 3D printer, a laser cutter, and the ability to fabricate almost anything a client needed. We were good at it. But something was missing.

What we really wanted to make was objects that belonged somewhere permanently. Not client deliverables. Not service outputs. Things with their own quiet authority, things that change the feeling of a room by their presence alone.

So we repositioned. The machines are still here. The precision is still here. But now we use them to make our own collection: Createwith products, designed and made in Norway, in small batches.

Walnut and brass

We chose walnut and brass because they are honest. They do not pretend. Walnut has grain, variation, warmth. No two pieces are the same. Brass tarnishes slightly at touch points, brightening with use. These are not defects. They are the evidence that something is real.

Both materials age in the direction of beauty. A walnut piece handled daily for twenty years is richer than a new one. A brass fitting that has seen ten Norwegian winters has a quality no factory finish can replicate.

Small batches

We make a limited number of each piece. This is not a marketing strategy. It is the only way to maintain the standard we require of ourselves, and the only way to give each object the attention it deserves during manufacture.

We do not have inventory. When you order, we make. That typically means a lead time of four to eight weeks. We think that is a reasonable price for something you will keep for thirty years.

Connected to Designwith

Createwith is part of the same creative family as Designwith, a design and fabrication studio that handles larger commissions, architectural installations, and custom one-offs. If your project requires something beyond our standard collection, Designwith is where that conversation begins.
